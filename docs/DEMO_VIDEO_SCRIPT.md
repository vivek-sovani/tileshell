# TileShell — full feature tour (recording script)

Every feature, grouped the way the app itself groups them (same taxonomy as
the in-app "what you can do" screen), in the order a new user would
naturally discover them: land on Start → see it's alive → learn to edit it →
organize into folders/stacks → check notifications → explore the other
swipe destinations → personalize it → back it up → lock it → the
privacy/accessibility close.

**15 sections, ~9–12 minutes recorded straight through** at a natural pace.
Each section is self-contained — record them as separate takes and stitch,
or do it in one continuous pass. Each also works as its own standalone short
clip if you'd rather post a multi-part series instead of one long video (see
the note at the very end).

Every section has: **Setup** (state you need before hitting record, if any),
**Beats** (action → say), and a runtime estimate. Rewrite the "say" lines in
your own voice — they're a scaffold, not a teleprompter script.

## 1. Start screen basics
*Runtime: ~30s*

**Setup:** A layout with a mix of tile sizes (small/medium/wide/large) so the
grid density is visible at a glance.

**Beats:**
- Static beat on Start. *"This is TileShell — the Windows Phone Start screen,
  rebuilt for Android."*
- Slow scroll down the full grid. *"Four tile sizes — small, medium, wide,
  and large — and any app can be large, on any grid density."*
- Open Personalize → tap grid columns 4 → 5 → 6, showing the grid reflow live.
  *"Pack 4, 5, or 6 tiles across a row — your call."*

## 2. Live tiles deep dive
*Runtime: ~60–75s — the heart of the demo*

**Setup:** Pin one of each: clock, weather, calendar, a music app, and (if
granted) people/photos, plus an app with a pending notification (mail or
messages) for the badge/preview shots.

**Beats:**
- Point at the clock tile. *"Clock — live time, date, weekday. Flip it and
  it leads with your next alarm."*
- Point at weather. *"Real forecasts, no account needed — temperature,
  condition, high and low."*
- Point at calendar. *"Always shows today's date; flips to your next event
  once you grant calendar access."*
- Play music in the background, show the tile. *"Now-playing with album art
  and transport controls — tap play/pause right on the tile."*
- (If contacts granted) show the people tile mosaic cycling photos. *"A
  rotating mosaic of your contacts' photos."*
- (If photos picked) show the photos tile cross-fading. *"Or a slideshow of
  photos you pick yourself."*
- Show a mail/message tile flip to reveal sender + snippet. *"Mail and
  messages show the sender and a preview — no need to open the app."*
- Show any other app tile with a pending notification flip to a preview.
  *"Actually — any app does this, not just mail. Pin anything, and if it has
  a notification, the tile shows it."*

## 3. Editing & organizing
*Runtime: ~30–40s*

**Beats:**
- Long-press a tile. *"Long-press any tile to enter edit mode."*
- Drag it to reorder. *"Drag to reorder — the rest of the grid flows around
  it."*
- Tap the resize handle a couple of times, cycling sizes. *"Tap the resize
  handle to cycle through sizes."*
- Tap the colour dot, pick a different accent, then show "tile colour from
  app icon" picking the app's own dominant colour. *"Give any tile its own
  colour — pick an accent, or pull the colour straight from the app's icon."*
- Drag a tile into the empty space below the last row. *"Drag below the
  last row to send a tile to the bottom."*

## 4. Folders
*Runtime: ~30–40s*

**Beats:**
- Drag one tile onto another, centre to centre, pause, release. *"Line two
  tiles up centre to centre and hold — they merge into a folder."*
- Tap the folder. *"Tap it and it opens right in place — no separate
  screen."*
- Show resizing/recolouring a child inside the open folder, then tap × to
  unpin one back to Start. *"Everything you can do to a top-level tile, you
  can do to one inside a folder — resize, recolour, or send it back to
  Start."*
- Tap the folder again to collapse. *"Tap again to collapse."*
- (If you have several apps of one category) show Personalize's
  auto-create-category-folders option. *"Or let TileShell suggest a whole
  folder for you, grouped by category."*

## 5. Widget stacks
*Runtime: ~45–60s*

**Setup:** Have two large tiles ready to merge (or a folder whose members
you can resize to wide/large on camera).

**Beats:**
- Merge two large tiles together. *"Merge two large tiles instead, and you
  get a widget stack — a full-size live tile carousel."*
- Let it auto-rotate a few seconds, then swipe near the right edge to flip
  manually. *"It rotates through members on its own, every ten seconds — or
  swipe the right edge to flip instantly."*
- Tap a music/notification member to show its live face still works inside
  the stack. *"Every live face — music controls, notification previews —
  stays fully interactive inside the stack."*
- Select it in edit mode, tap the folder-icon corner control to expand it.
  *"Open it up to manage members individually."*
- Point out the "make · large stack" (or wide) and "back to folder" actions
  next to the members. *"Switch between wide and large directly, or turn it
  back into a plain folder — it's never one accidental tap from
  converting."*
- Tap × on one member. *"Pull a member out and it goes right back to Start."*

## 6. Notifications & badges
*Runtime: ~20–30s*

**Setup:** Have at least one pinned app, one folder, and one widget stack
each with a pending notification, so all three badge styles are visible.

**Beats:**
- Point at a plain tile's badge. *"Every tile shows its own unread count."*
- Open a folder, point at the combined total by its name and the per-app
  badge on the one icon that actually has something pending. *"A folder
  shows both — its combined total, and which app inside it actually has
  something waiting."*
- Show the same on a widget stack member as it rotates. *"Same thing on a
  stack — each member carries its own badge as it rotates through."*
- Swipe to the App List and point at an unpinned app showing up under
  "recent" with a badge. *"Even an app you haven't pinned shows up here if
  it has something pending."*

## Splitting into a series instead

If a single ~10-minute video feels long for your format, each numbered
section above is written to stand alone — post them as a numbered series
("TileShell, part 3: widget stacks") instead of one long video. Good
candidates for their own short-form clip on their own: **2 (live tiles)**,
**5 (widget stacks)**, and **12 (personalization)** — they're the most
visually distinctive and least dependent on the others for context.

## Filming notes
- Set up section 2's tiles (music playing, a notification pending on
  mail/messages) *before* you hit record — waiting for a real notification
  to arrive on camera is dead air.
- Section 4/5's merge gesture needs a deliberate, held drag — line up centre
  to centre and pause a beat before releasing, or it reads as a reorder.
- Keep tile arrangement on "auto-arrange" while filming sections 3–5 so
  moving/removing tiles doesn't leave a visible gap (Windows-Phone "sticky"
  mode deliberately leaves one, which reads as a bug if you don't explain
  it).
- Section 10's two-finger swipe can be fiddly on the first try — retake
  rather than fight with it on camera.
